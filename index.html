Quick Start Guide

Montages

Montage Definition. A montage is a sequence of short video clips edited together to tell a story, compress time, or highlight contrast. It’s one of the most effective tools in visual storytelling, used to evoke emotion or deliver meaning quickly.

Montages’ Place in Filmography. From silent films to music videos, montages have shaped how we watch and feel movies. They let filmmakers jump through time, build tension, or convey growth—all in seconds. Even simple montages can elevate everyday moments into something memorable.

Montage Creation. Traditionally, montages require recording many clips, organizing them, and editing them by hand. This process takes time, tools, and experience. For most people, the editing wall is where good video ideas go to die.

Montaj

Why Montaj? Montaj removes that wall. It lets you record clips live, one by one, and builds the montage as you go. The app handles the transitions, audio blending, and timing—no editing software needed. You focus on the moments; Montaj shapes the story.

Why a Minimalist App? Montaj is built to disappear. The interface is simple so you can stay focused on recording, not navigating menus. Whether you're capturing a trip, telling a story, or documenting your day, Montaj keeps the process light and fast.
